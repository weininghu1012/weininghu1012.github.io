ABOUT ME

Hi! My name is Weining Hu and I just graduated from University of British Columbia (UBC) in Computer Science combined with Statistics. In September, I will come back to UBC to pursue a master degree in statistics.



In the summer of 2016, I worked as a software engineer intern at Amazon AWS. The project I worked on would enable the automation of webpage content update without the need for code deployment.



In the summer of 2015, I worked as a research assistant at Statistics department at UBC under the supervison of Prof. Wu Lang and Prof.Paul Gustafson. The research work I was doing focused on the analysis of clinical trials and longitudinal datasets. Medical data can sometimes be messy and incomplete and therefore I also develop interest in methods dealing with censored data, sparse data and nonparametric methods.

In my spare time, I like to play around with data visualization tools. Thanks to my background in computer science, I have more opportunities to try many different tools and languages to see what combination could present the best visualization effect and maybe reveal the fact that was ignored before.
